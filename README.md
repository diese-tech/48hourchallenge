# I Died and Woke Up a Support Main

*a moba story*

A frontend-focused browser game created for the A3 Web Design Challenge.

## Project Summary

**I Died and Woke Up a Support Main** is a reactive side-scrolling support survival game where the player protects a dysfunctional MOBA team against escalating waves of enemies in a collapsing cosmic battlefield.

The player is not the carry.

The player enables whoever is currently carrying.

Core fantasy:

> Hold reality together while your teammates attempt to throw the match.

## Challenge Context

This project is being built for a **frontend web development challenge**.

The goal is not to build a production MMO, multiplayer MOBA, backend-heavy live-service game, or large RPG system.

The goal is to create:

- a memorable browser-playable experience
- a polished gameplay loop
- a strong visual identity
- responsive frontend interaction design
- readable side-scrolling gameplay
- high-impact UI, motion, and presentation
- a technically clean frontend implementation

## Game Concept

The game is a side-scrolling survival experience inspired by MOBA support gameplay, lightweight roguelite progression, and dramatic anime/esports framing.

The player controls **The Last Support**, a celestial battlefield stabilizer responsible for keeping four autonomous teammates alive as enemy waves escalate.

Teammates fight on their own, make questionable decisions, and occasionally enter powerful **Shine States** where they become the temporary win condition. The player must identify who is currently carrying and enable them before the team collapses.

## Core Loop

1. Enemy waves attack from the right side of the battlefield.
2. Teammates fight autonomously.
3. The player heals, shields, repositions, and stabilizes the team.
4. XP is earned through successful support actions.
5. Level-ups pause gameplay and offer upgrade choices.
6. The player selects upgrades that alter their support style.
7. Survive 10 minutes to win.
8. Endless mode begins after victory.
9. The team eventually falls under overwhelming pressure.

## Match Structure

- **Victory condition:** survive for 10 minutes
- **Endless mode:** begins after the 10-minute win state
- **Failure condition:** full team wipe
- **Progression:** XP bar with upgrade choices
- **Upgrade cap:** roughly 6 impactful upgrades per run

## Player Character

### The Last Support

The player character is a mysterious celestial support entity and the final surviving battlefield stabilizer in a collapsing MOBA universe.

The Last Support is not a traditional healer priest or fantasy cleric. They are a calm, exhausted, adaptive, divine tactical entity designed to feel like the operating system of the battlefield.

Visual themes:

- white
- cyan
- pale gold
- soft violet
- translucent energy
- holographic UI fragments
- glowing support sigils
- celestial hard-light shields
- orbiting support constructs
- healing ribbon trails

Core abilities:

- primary heal
- shield
- mobility skill
- ultimate stabilization ability

## Team Archetypes

### Solo — The Knight

A king-arthur-style frontline knight inspired by heroic MOBA bruisers.

- frontline anchor
- heroic overcommitter
- survives impossible pressure
- creates offscreen emergencies
- gold, steel, and orange color language

### ADC — The Elf Ranger

An agile elf ranger using dual repeater crossbows.

- fragile hypercarry
- sustained ranged damage
- high mobility
- strongest when protected
- purple, silver, and black color language

### Mid — The Flame Humanoid

A humanoid fire/lava entity inspired by battlefield artillery mages.

- unstable magical burst damage
- screen-clearing power spikes
- vulnerable while channeling
- red, orange, magma, and lava-black color language

### Jungler — The Rogue

A stealth rogue inspired by elusive assassin archetypes.

- roaming opportunist
- unpredictable momentum swinger
- sudden assassination pressure
- green, shadow black, and muted gray color language

## Shine State System

Each teammate periodically enters a **Shine State**.

During Shine State:

- their effectiveness increases dramatically
- battlefield priority shifts toward protecting or enabling them
- visual and audio presentation intensifies
- the player must quickly adapt their support decisions

Examples:

- Solo enters a berserk frontline state
- ADC enters an overclocked firing mode
- Mid enters arcane focus
- Jungler enters an ambush chain state

Core design principle:

> The support adapts to whoever is currently carrying.

## Progression Philosophy

Progression should change support playstyle, not just increase numbers.

Preferred upgrade styles:

- chain healing
- projectile reflection
- revive mechanics
- battlefield zones
- cooldown manipulation
- sacrifice mechanics
- ally-specific synergies

Avoid:

- tiny stat boosts
- passive number inflation
- complex inventory systems
- traditional MOBA item shops

## Enemy Philosophy

Enemy count should remain controlled and readable.

Target enemy roster:

- melee swarmers
- artillery units
- assassins
- tanks
- support disruptors
- minibosses

Priority:

> Readability over complexity.
